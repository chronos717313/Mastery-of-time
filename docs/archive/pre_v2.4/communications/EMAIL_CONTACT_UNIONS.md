**Background**

I am developing an alternative theoretical framework called the "Theory of Time Mastery" (Théorie de Maîtrise du Temps) that proposes to explain dark matter phenomena through temporal distortion fields created by massive objects. The theory makes a specific, testable prediction that distinguishes it from Lambda-CDM:

**Prediction:** Dark matter halos should be asymmetric and preferentially aligned toward neighboring massive galaxies, with a measurable correlation between halo orientation (θ_halo) and the direction to the most massive neighbor (θ_neighbor).

**Quantitatively:**
- Time Mastery predicts: Correlation(θ_halo, θ_neighbor) > 0.5
- Lambda-CDM predicts: Correlation ≈ 0 (random orientation)

**Connection to Your Work**

Your UNIONS measurement of halo ellipticity **e = 0.46 ± 0.10** is particularly intriguing, as this value:
1. Exceeds typical Lambda-CDM simulation predictions (e ~ 0.2-0.3)
2. Falls within the range predicted by Time Mastery theory (e ~ 0.3-0.6)
3. Provides exactly the type of weak lensing data needed to test the orientation prediction

**Proposed Analysis**

I would like to perform a correlation analysis using UNIONS weak lensing data combined with SDSS galaxy catalogs:

**Method:**
- Select galaxies from UNIONS with reliable weak lensing measurements (S/N > 3)
- Identify massive neighbors (M > 10¹¹ M☉, distance 0.5-2 Mpc) using SDSS
- Calculate correlation between halo position angles and directions to neighbors
- Perform statistical tests (Pearson correlation, von Mises distribution, bootstrap)

**Expected sample size:** ~1,000-2,000 galaxies
